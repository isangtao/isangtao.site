Apples are one of the most widely cultivated tree fruits, known for their crisp texture and sweet or tart flavor. They come in a variety of colors, including red, green, and yellow. Apples are rich in fiber, vitamin C, and various antioxidants. They can be eaten raw, cooked into sauces and pies, or fermented into cider.

Bananas are long, curved fruits with a soft, creamy flesh and a yellow peel when ripe. They are an excellent source of potassium, vitamin B6, and vitamin C. Bananas are a convenient and portable snack, and they are also used in smoothies, baking, and desserts.

Oranges are citrus fruits known for their bright orange color and juicy, sweet-tart pulp. They are a fantastic source of vitamin C, which is essential for a healthy immune system. Oranges are typically peeled and eaten fresh or juiced. The zest of the orange peel can also be used in cooking and baking to add flavor.

Strawberries are small, heart-shaped fruits that are bright red when ripe. They have a sweet, slightly tart flavor and a juicy texture, with tiny seeds on their outer surface. Strawberries are packed with vitamin C and manganese and are often enjoyed fresh, in desserts, or made into jams and jellies.

Blueberries are small, round, and blue-to-purple berries. They are praised for their high antioxidant content, particularly anthocyanins, which give them their color. Blueberries have a sweet and slightly tangy taste and are often eaten fresh, added to cereals and yogurts, or baked into muffins and pancakes.

Watermelon is a large, heavy fruit with a green rind and a juicy, red or pink flesh filled with black seeds. As its name suggests, it has a high water content, making it incredibly refreshing, especially in hot weather. It's a good source of vitamins C and A.

Pineapples are tropical fruits with a tough, spiky outer skin and sweet, golden-yellow flesh. They have a distinctive sweet and tart flavor. Pineapples are a great source of vitamin C and manganese. They can be eaten fresh, grilled, or used in a variety of sweet and savory dishes.

Mangoes are tropical stone fruits with a sweet, fragrant, and juicy flesh. Their skin color ranges from green to red to yellow-orange. Mangoes are rich in vitamins A, C, and E. They are delicious eaten on their own, in smoothies, or as part of salads and chutneys.

grps grow in clusters on vines and come in various colors, including green, red, and black. They can be sweet or tart and are eaten fresh, dried to make raisins, or fermented to produce wine. Grapes are a good source of vitamins C and K.

Peaches are soft, fuzzy-skinned fruits with a sweet, juicy, and aromatic flesh. They have a single large seed in the center. Peaches are a good source of vitamins C and A. They are wonderful when eaten fresh, grilled, or baked into cobblers and pies.

Durian (/ˈdʊəriən/ ⓘ[1]) is the edible fruit of several tree species belonging to the genus Durio. There are 30 recognized species, at least nine of which produce edible fruit.[2] Durio zibethinus, native to Borneo and Sumatra, is the only species available on the international market. It has over 300 named varieties in Thailand and over 200 in Malaysia as of 2021. Other species are sold in their local regions
